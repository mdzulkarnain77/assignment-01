Dr. Muhammad Yunus, born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, Bangladesh, is an esteemed economist and social entrepreneur, best known for founding the Grameen Bank in 1983. His innovative microcredit model, which provides small loans to the impoverished without requiring collateral, has empowered millions to escape poverty. Dr. Yunus was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for his impactful work in economic and social development. His contributions have revolutionized microfinance, making a significant difference in the lives of countless individuals and inspiring similar initiatives worldwide. Dr. Yunus continues to be a leading advocate for social business and economic empowerment.
